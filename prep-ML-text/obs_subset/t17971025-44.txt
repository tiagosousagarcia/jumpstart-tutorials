Look at that letter and bill of parcels, whose hand-writing are they?
The prisoner's; I put the bill of parcels within side a parcel, and he came and took it from me. I gave it to him for Lee.
Who was that Lee?
Thomas Griffith , an apprentice of Mr. Wrench's; that bill of parcels has my hand-writing upon it.
Where did you get that parcel from in which you included that bill of parcels?
Elizabeth Tolley brought it to me from North, and some lines with it, certifying that it did come from him.
I carry out parcels for Messrs. Wrench. The prisoner sent me with a paper parcel to the last witness; it was on a Tuesday night, the week before North was taken, he was taken the Saturday following.
You know nothing about it, but that it was a paper parcel?
Nothing more.
I am a grocer. The prisoner brought me a parcel on Friday morning, October 6, to go to Mr. George Lee, at Hertford, I sent a bill of parcels with the goods that I sent at the same time.
Do you know where he lived?
No.
You supposed that he lived at Hertford?
I recollect now it was at Ware, near the Spread Eagle.
I am a constable; I searched the house of George Lee, at Ware; his real name is Thomas Griffiths .
Look at that letter, and tell me if you found that?
I found this at Robertson's house.
Look at those other two papers?
These were enclosed in the parcels at Lee's house.
Every article corresponds with the bill of parcels.
