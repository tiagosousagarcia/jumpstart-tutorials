You are a cheesemonger .
I am, in Bishopsgate street, the prisoner was my servant and warehouseman; he was sent by me on Tuesday the 11th of March, to the west end of the town to recieve orders.
He was entrusted to receive money for you.
He was.
On the 12th of March did he bring any money to you from Mr. Lee's.
He did not.
Mr. Lee was a customer of yours.
He is, he was indebted to me seventeen pounds eight shillings; he is a cheesemonger, in Hungerford-street.
That man has lived with you for some years.
He has been with me about eight years, and five years he has lived in the house.
The man at the bar was employed for the purpose of receiving orders and taking money.
Yes.
Mr. Lee was a customer of yours.
He was.
Your servant returned after receiving orders, and then there is an entry made in some book.
Yes.
That book is not here.
It is not; when he comes home he brings an account of what he has received, that is his memorandum (producing a paper) of his own writing, one side is the orders, and on the reverse side is the money that he has received, but no mention is made of receiving any from Mr. Lee.
I am a cheesemonger, I live at No. 10, Hungerford-street.
On the 12th of March, did you pay the prisoner any sum of money on account of his master.
Yes, I paid him seventeen pounds eight shillings, on account of his master, George Wood , I paid him a ten pound Henly bank note, a five pound Henly bank note, and two bank of England notes of one pound each, and eight shillings in cash.
You are one of the clerks of the prosecutor.
Yes.
Did the prisoner account to you for this sum of money.
He never did.
How many clerks are there in your house.
Only Mr. Everest and myself.
You are also a clerk, did he ever account to you for this money.
Never.
(to prosecutor) When was he taken up.
On the 17th of April.
Was seventeen pounds eight shillings due to you from Mr. Lee.
Yes; the way I discovered it was paid was by one of my clerks calling on the Monday for the money, then it appeared that he had paid it.
(to Lee) Before the 17th of March you were indebted to Mr. Wood, seventeen pounds eight shillings.
Yes.
